About me

I am Alisa Rohalska, a Switzerland-based hobbyphotographer with Ukrainian roots and a lifelong love for capturing real moments. My journey started with film cameras, continued through everyday smartphone storytelling, and today lives in digital photography focused on emotion over perfection.


In a world full of filters and AI-generated images, I choose authenticity: natural textures, gentle color grading in Lightroom, and portraits that preserve who you are instead of turning you into someone else. Life is too short to hide your true self – my work is about celebrating your personality and unique presence.


I also regularly create self‑portraits to stay connected with what my clients feel in front of the camera. Stepping into both roles – photographer and subject – helps me explore vulnerability from different angles and stay honest in the way I guide and portray others.
